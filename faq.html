Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I miss a dose?

Do not double your dose unless your healthcare provider tells you to. Ask your doctor or pharmacist for instructions that match your medication.

Source: CDC – Treatment

Can I stop my medication if I feel better?

No. Feeling better does not always mean diabetes is gone. Stopping medication without advice can cause blood sugar to rise again.

What foods should I avoid?

Focus on a balanced eating plan. Limit sugary drinks, highly processed foods, and excessive refined carbohydrates. A dietitian or healthcare worker can help personalize your plan.

Source: CDC – Healthy Eating

How often should I check my blood sugar?

This depends on your treatment plan. People using insulin may need to check more often. Follow instructions from your clinic.

Where can I find more support?

Use trusted organizations such as WHO, CDC, IDF, and your local health clinic.
